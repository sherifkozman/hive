# Meridian Software Inc. — Investment Committee Work-Up
**B2B SaaS, vertical ERP | All figures USD thousands unless noted | Prepared for IC review, valuation date 2026-01-01**

---

## Executive Summary (BLUF)

**Valuation range:** DCF intrinsic value of **$18.75–$27.70 per share** across a WACC (9.06%–11.06%) × terminal-growth (2.0%–3.0%) sensitivity grid, with a **central estimate of $22.34/share** (WACC 10.06%, terminal growth 2.5%; enterprise value $195.0M, equity value $205.5M on 9.2M diluted shares). This DCF range should be read as a **fundamentals-based floor**: applying public/private SaaS ARR multiples (8–15x ARR for moderate-growth names) to Meridian's ~$71.5M beginning-2026 ARR implies an enterprise value of roughly $572M–$1,073M — several multiples above the DCF. The gap is explained by the long-duration growth-deceleration profile, heavy reinvestment (capex + NWC) embedded in unlevered FCF, and a terminal value that carries 79% of enterprise value (above the 80% caution line in our methodology, i.e. right at the edge of over-reliance on terminal assumptions).

**Key risks:**
1. **Terminal value dominance (79% of EV)** — small changes in WACC/terminal growth swing per-share value by ~40% (see sensitivity table).
2. **Growth-plan inconsistency** — the bottom-up, bookings/ARR-driven FY2026 revenue forecast implies **32.5% growth (base case)**, materially above the **25% growth baked into the DCF's fade schedule** — which in fact lines up almost exactly with our bookings-driven **bear case (25.2%)**. Management's true operating plan needs to be pinned down.
3. **Leverage/liquidity improved quickly but off a small equity base** — D/E fell from 1.53x (2024) to 0.64x (2025) and interest coverage rose from 4.96x to 15.02x, but ROE (42.3%) is still leverage-amplified (DuPont) rather than purely margin-driven.

**3 focal points for the committee:**
1. **Reconcile the growth plan** — is 25% (DCF) or ~32% (bookings walk) the right FY2026 revenue growth assumption? This alone moves the valuation and the credibility of both models.
2. **Stress the terminal value** — with TV at 79% of EV, request management's view on post-2030 competitive durability and sustainable margin ceiling before anchoring on the DCF point estimate.
3. **Track Rule of 40 trajectory** — Meridian is just under the 40 threshold (37.9% in 2024, 38.9% in 2025); it is trading growth for margin, which is the right direction but not yet at the SaaS efficiency bar.

## 3. Driver-Based 2026 Revenue Forecast

### 3.1 Methodology and Stated Assumptions

Inputs provided: beginning 2026 ARR $71,500; Q1 new-bookings pipeline $6,800; quarterly bookings growth 6%; annual gross churn 8%; net revenue retention (NRR) 112%; 90-day average sales cycle; quarterly headcount plan (312→355).

**Modeling assumptions (stated explicitly since the raw inputs leave gaps):**
- Q1 pipeline of $6,800 is treated as **fully converting to closed-won new ARR bookings in Q1** (100% conversion) for the base case; bull/bear scenarios flex this conversion rate.
- NRR (112% annual) is converted to a quarterly-compounding factor: (1.12)^(1/4) ≈ 1.0289 per quarter, applied to the existing ARR book (this already nets expansion, contraction, and churn together).
- **Reconciliation check:** NRR − 1 + gross churn = 12% + 8% = **20% implied annual gross expansion/upsell rate** on the existing book — a useful internal-consistency check on the given inputs, since NRR and gross churn were both supplied independently.
- Quarterly ARR walk: ARR(end of quarter) = ARR(begin)×1.0289 + new bookings added in the quarter.
- Quarterly recognized revenue is approximated as the **average of beginning and ending ARR for the quarter, divided by 4** (i.e., ARR is assumed to ramp roughly linearly through the quarter before being recognized as revenue) — a standard simplification, not a GAAP waterfall.
- Headcount plan (312→355 FTEs, $168K fully-loaded cost/head) is provided but is a cost driver, not a revenue driver, and is not required to build the revenue forecast per the task scope; it is noted here for the committee's cost-model follow-up.

### 3.4 Reconciliation with the DCF Growth Assumption

A notable finding: the DCF's 2026 revenue growth assumption (25.0%) is **almost identical to the bookings-driven bear case (25.2%)**, and well below the base case (32.5%) built directly from the given bookings/NRR/churn drivers. Two readings are possible, and the committee should ask management which is closer to the operating plan:
- The DCF is conservative by design (appropriately, for a long-horizon valuation), and the bookings walk's 100%-pipeline-conversion assumption is optimistic.
- Alternatively, the bookings-driven base case is closer to the real plan, in which case the DCF may be **understating near-term cash flow** — worth a follow-up DCF run with a 30%+ 2026 growth assumption as an upside case.

## 4. Key Data Gaps and Assumptions (Summary)

- No FY2023 balance sheet was provided — liquidity, leverage, efficiency ratios, ROE, and DuPont are only available for FY2024–FY2025 (two points, not a three-year trend).
- Quick ratio assumes zero inventory (reasonable for a SaaS company) and conservatively excludes "other current assets" from quick assets.
- 2026 ARR-to-revenue timing uses an average-ARR/4 approximation, not a contractual revenue-recognition waterfall.
- Bull/bear churn and NRR assumptions are analyst-constructed (only the base case was given in the fixture) and should be validated against management's own downside/upside planning inputs.
- WACC/CAPM inputs, revenue growth fade, EBIT margin path, D&A/capex/NWC ratios, terminal growth, net debt, and share count are all taken directly from the provided `dcf_assumptions` block; no independent comparable-company work was performed (out of scope per the provided data), which is why the EV/Revenue sanity check is flagged as directional only.
