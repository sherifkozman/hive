# Meridian Software Inc. — Investment Committee Work-Up
*B2B SaaS, vertical ERP | All figures USD thousands unless per-share | Prepared for IC review*

## 0. Executive Summary (BLUF)

**Bottom line:** Meridian is a ~$68.3M-revenue vertical-ERP SaaS business that crossed into profitability in FY2024 and is now compounding growth (+25% in FY2025) alongside expanding margins (EBITDA margin 13.9% in FY2025, up from -4.1% in FY2023) and a net-cash balance sheet. On a 5-year FCFF DCF, **base-case equity value is ~$205.5M, or ~$22.34/share**, with a sensitivity range of **~$18.75–$27.70/share** (~$162M–$244M enterprise value) across ±1pt WACC and ±0.5pt terminal growth. The implied EV/Revenue multiple (2.9x trailing, 2.3x forward) sits in a defensible-but-not-cheap band for a 25%-growth, ~14%-EBITDA-margin SaaS asset — reasonable relative to Rule-of-40 performance (~39 in FY2025) but dependent on the margin-expansion glide path holding.

**Valuation range for IC:** **$18.75 – $27.70/share** (point estimate $22.34), driven primarily by terminal-value assumptions (~79% of enterprise value sits in the terminal value — standard for a 5-year explicit window but a key sensitivity to interrogate).

**Key risks:**
1. **Terminal-value concentration & margin execution risk** — ~79% of EV comes from the terminal year; the DCF requires EBIT margin to expand from 11% to 18% over 5 years (a ~1.75pt/yr step-up) while growth decelerates from 25% to 10%. Any slippage in either compresses value disproportionately (see sensitivity table).
2. **Customer concentration / retention durability** — the 2026 forecast embeds a strong 112% NRR alongside 8% gross churn (implying ~20% gross expansion). These are healthy SaaS metrics, but the fixture provides no cohort-level detail; a modest NRR miss (e.g., 105%) pulls FY2026 revenue down ~4%.
3. **Balance-sheet data gap** — no FY2023 balance sheet was provided, so FY2024 ROE/turnover/DSO trends rely on ending (not average) balances and 2023 leverage/liquidity ratios cannot be computed; this is a diligence follow-up item, not a red flag, since 2024–2025 figures reconcile perfectly.

**Three focal points for the committee:**
- **Interrogate the margin-expansion assumption** (11%→18% EBIT margin by 2030) against Meridian's actual cost structure — is the S&M/R&D leverage embedded in the plan realistic given the headcount build (312→355 FTEs in 2026 alone)?
- **Stress-test terminal value** — given ~79% of EV is terminal, consider whether an EV/EBITDA-multiple exit cross-check (not provided in the fixture) would corroborate or challenge the Gordon-growth terminal value.
- **Revenue-forecast reconciliation** — the bottoms-up driver-based FY2026 build ($83.9M base case) lands ~1.6% below the DCF's top-down 25%-growth assumption ($85.3M); worth confirming which the operating plan should anchor to.

## 3. Driver-Based 2026 Revenue Forecast (Quarterly)

**Method & stated assumptions:**
- Beginning ARR (1/1/2026): $71,500.
- New bookings closed per quarter (Q1 = $6,800, growing at the given 6% Q/Q rate thereafter): Q1 $6,800 / Q2 $7,208 / Q3 $7,640 / Q4 $8,099.
- **Assumption:** given the ~90-day (one-quarter) average sales cycle, bookings closed in a quarter begin contributing to the ARR run-rate starting the *following* quarter (a one-quarter implementation/ramp lag) — this is not explicit in the fixture and is stated here for reproducibility.
- Gross churn of 8% annually is applied evenly, 2.0%/quarter, against beginning-of-quarter ARR.
- NRR of 112% is decomposed as: **implied gross expansion/upsell = NRR − 1 + gross churn = 20.0% annually** (5.0%/quarter), applied against beginning-of-quarter ARR, so that churn + expansion nets to the stated 112% NRR.
- Quarterly recognized revenue is approximated as **average-of-quarter ARR ÷ 4** (i.e., (beginning + ending ARR)/2, divided by 4), a standard approximation for ratably-recognized subscription revenue.

|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|

## 4. Summary of Assumptions & Data Gaps (for reproducibility)

1. FY2023 balance sheet was not provided; FY2023 liquidity/leverage/ROE/turnover ratios and FY2024 average-balance metrics are not computable and are omitted rather than estimated.
2. FY2024/FY2025 balance sheets each foot exactly (assets = liabilities + equity), and EBIT − interest reconciles to net income + tax in all three income-statement years — no fixture inconsistencies found.
3. Bookings-to-ARR contribution timing (one-quarter lag) is a stated modeling assumption, not given explicitly in the fixture.
4. Churn and expansion are assumed to apply evenly across quarters (annual rate ÷ 4); no seasonality was specified in the fixture.
5. Bull/bear driver deltas (bookings growth, churn, NRR, Q1 bookings) are assumptions layered on top of the given base-case drivers for scenario analysis purposes.
6. No market comparable-company data was provided; the EV/Revenue sanity check is an internal-consistency check only, not a market-calibrated benchmark.
