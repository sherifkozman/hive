
# Valuation Ratios & Ratio-Analysis Discipline

## Valuation Ratios (market value relative to financial metrics)

**Price-to-Earnings (P/E)** = Share Price / EPS. Most widely used; high P/E suggests growth expectations or overvaluation. Use trailing (TTM) and forward P/E. Benchmarks: <10x value, 10–20x fair, 20–35x growth, >35x premium.

**Price-to-Book (P/B)** = Share Price / Book Value Per Share. Market vs accounting value; below 1.0 may indicate undervaluation or distress; most useful for asset-heavy industries. Benchmarks: <1.0 undervalued, 1.0–2.5 fair, 2.5–5.0 premium, >5.0 rich.

**Price-to-Sales (P/S)** = Market Cap / Revenue. Useful for companies without positive earnings; compare within industry; lower = potentially better value. Benchmarks: <1.0 value, 1.0–3.0 fair, 3.0–8.0 growth, >8.0 premium.

**EV/EBITDA** = Enterprise Value / EBITDA. Capital-structure-neutral; preferred for M&A and LBOs; more comparable across capital structures than P/E. Benchmarks: <6x value, 6–12x fair, 12–20x growth, >20x premium.

**PEG Ratio** = P/E / Earnings Growth Rate (%). Growth-adjusted P/E; 1.0 suggests fair valuation relative to growth. Benchmarks: <0.5 undervalued, 0.5–1.0 fair, 1.0–2.0 fully valued, >2.0 overvalued.

## Ratio-Analysis Best Practices

1. **Compare within industry** — ratios vary significantly across sectors.
2. **Analyze trends** — a single snapshot is insufficient; look at 3–5 year trends.
3. **Use multiple ratios** — no single ratio tells the complete story.
4. **Consider context** — accounting policies, business cycle, and company stage matter.
5. **DuPont decomposition** — break ROE into margin × turnover × leverage.
6. **Peer comparison** — compare against direct competitors, not just broad benchmarks.
7. **Watch for manipulation** — revenue-recognition changes, off-balance-sheet items, and one-time adjustments can distort ratios.

These valuation multiples double as sanity checks on DCF outputs: an implied EV/EBITDA or P/E far outside peer ranges signals inconsistent DCF assumptions.
