
# Liquidity & Leverage Ratios

## Liquidity (ability to meet short-term obligations)

**Current Ratio** = Current Assets / Current Liabilities. Short-term solvency; too high may mean inefficient asset use, too low signals liquidity risk. Benchmarks: <1.0 concern, 1.0–1.5 acceptable, 1.5–3.0 healthy, >3.0 excessive.

**Quick Ratio (Acid Test)** = (Current Assets − Inventory) / Current Liabilities. More conservative — excludes inventory (least liquid current asset); critical where inventory is slow-moving. Benchmarks: <0.8 concern, 0.8–1.0 acceptable, 1.0–2.0 healthy, >2.0 excessive.

**Cash Ratio** = Cash & Equivalents / Current Liabilities. Most conservative measure; ability to pay with cash on hand, important during credit crunches. Benchmarks: <0.2 low, 0.2–0.5 adequate, 0.5–1.0 strong, >1.0 excessive.

## Leverage (extent of debt financing)

**Debt-to-Equity** = Total Debt / Total Shareholders' Equity. Financial leverage and risk; norms vary widely (utilities vs tech). Benchmarks: <0.3 conservative, 0.3–0.8 moderate, 0.8–2.0 elevated, >2.0 high risk.

**Interest Coverage** = Operating Income (EBIT) / Interest Expense. Ability to service debt from operating earnings; below 1.5x is a lender red flag; critical for credit analysis. Benchmarks: <2.0 distressed, 2.0–5.0 adequate, 5.0–10.0 strong, >10.0 very strong.

**Debt Service Coverage Ratio (DSCR)** = Operating Cash Flow / Total Debt Service. Cash-based servicing capacity; includes principal repayments (unlike interest coverage); required by many loan covenants. Benchmarks: <1.0 default risk, 1.0–1.5 minimum, 1.5–2.5 comfortable, >2.5 strong.

## Application

Compare within industry — leverage norms differ sharply across sectors. Analyze 3–5 year trends rather than a single snapshot, and use these alongside profitability, efficiency, and valuation ratios. Watch for off-balance-sheet items and one-time adjustments that can distort the liquidity and leverage picture.
