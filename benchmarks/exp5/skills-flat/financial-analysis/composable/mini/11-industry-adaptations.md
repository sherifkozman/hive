
# Industry Adaptations

Sector-specific metrics, valuation multiples, and considerations. Apply the right lens per industry.

## SaaS / Software

**Metrics:** ARR/MRR growth; Net Revenue Retention (target >110%); CAC Payback (target <18 months); Rule of 40 (growth % + profit margin % ≥ 40%); LTV:CAC (target >3:1); gross margin (target >70%). **MRR/ARR decomposition:** new, expansion, contraction, churn; cohort-based forecasting for retention accuracy. **Valuation:** 5–15x ARR (growth-adjusted) — high-growth (>50%) 15–25x, moderate (20–50%) 8–15x, low (<20%) 3–8x. **Considerations:** deferred revenue recognition (ASC 606), stock-based comp impact on margins, cohort analysis critical.

## Retail / E-Commerce

**Metrics:** same-store sales growth (SSS, primary organic metric); gross margin by category; inventory turnover (grocery 14–20x, fashion 4–6x); revenue per square foot; CAC vs AOV; return-rate impact on unit economics. Use seasonal decomposition for monthly/weekly forecasts; markdown optimization affects gross margin; inventory turns drive working-capital forecasts. **Valuation:** EV/EBITDA 8–15x (premium brands higher), P/E 15–25x. **Considerations:** Q4 holiday concentration, working-capital intensity, omnichannel attribution.

## Manufacturing

**Metrics:** gross margin by product line; capacity utilization (target >80%); Days Inventory Outstanding (DIO); warranty reserve as % of revenue; CapEx % of revenue (maintenance vs growth); order backlog / book-to-bill. Order backlog is a leading indicator; capacity constraints create step-function cost increases; raw-material price forecasts drive COGS; utilization drives unit-cost projections. **Valuation:** EV/EBITDA 6–12x, P/E 12–20x. **Considerations:** raw-material volatility, supply-chain currency exposure, depreciation schedules (straight-line vs accelerated), regulatory compliance costs.

## Financial Services

**Metrics:** Net Interest Margin (NIM); ROE (target >12%); Cost-to-Income (target <60%); Non-Performing Loan (NPL) ratio; Tier 1 Capital Ratio; AUM growth. **Valuation:** P/B 1.0–2.5x, P/E 10–18x. **Considerations:** regulatory capital (Basel III/IV), interest-rate sensitivity, credit-risk provisioning (CECL / IFRS 9), mark-to-market vs held-to-maturity accounting.

## Healthcare

**Metrics:** revenue per patient/bed; payor mix (Medicare/Medicaid vs commercial); EBITDAR margin (rent-adjusted); clinical-trial pipeline value; patent-cliff exposure; R&D % of revenue (pharma benchmark 15–25%). **Valuation:** EV/EBITDA 10–18x (medtech), 12–20x (pharma); EV/Revenue 3–8x (services), 5–15x (devices). **Considerations:** reimbursement-rate changes, FDA approval timelines and probability-weighted pipeline, 340B pricing, medical-device regulation (MDR, QSR).
