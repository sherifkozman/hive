
# Comparable Companies, Precedent Transactions & Framework Selection

Use these market-based methods to sanity-check intrinsic (DCF) values and to value companies where DCF is weak.

## Comparable Company Analysis

Method: (1) select a peer group similar in size, industry, growth, and margins; (2) calculate trading multiples for each peer; (3) determine an appropriate multiple range; (4) apply to the target's metrics.

**Common multiples & when to use:**
- **EV/Revenue** — pre-profit companies, high-growth tech
- **EV/EBITDA** — most common for mature companies
- **EV/EBIT** — when D&A differs significantly across peers
- **P/E** — stable earnings, financial services
- **P/B** — banks, insurance, asset-heavy industries
- **EV/FCF** — capital-light businesses with clean FCF

**Peer selection criteria:** same/adjacent industry; size within 0.5x–2x of target revenue/market cap; same primary geography; similar growth (within 5–10%); similar operating-margin profile; comparable business model and customer base.

**Premium/discount adjustments:** higher growth → premium of 1–3x on EV/EBITDA; lower margins → discount of 1–2x; smaller scale → discount 10–20%; private company → discount 15–30% (illiquidity); control premium → 20–40% (acquisitions).

## Precedent Transaction Analysis

Method: identify comparable transactions in the same industry; calculate transaction multiples (EV/Revenue, EV/EBITDA); adjust for market conditions and deal-specific factors; apply to the target. Key considerations: transactions embed control premiums (typically 20–40%); market conditions at deal time affect multiples; strategic vs financial buyers value differently; consider embedded synergy expectations; more recent transactions carry greater relevance.

## Framework Selection

| Situation | Primary | Secondary |
|-----------|---------|-----------|
| Profitable, stable | DCF | Comparable companies |
| High growth, pre-profit | Comps (EV/Revenue) | DCF with scenarios |
| M&A target | Precedent transactions | DCF |
| Asset-heavy, cyclical | Asset-based | Normalized DCF |
| Financial institution | Dividend discount model | P/B, P/E comps |
| Distressed | Liquidation value | Restructured DCF |

Sanity check: DCF-implied multiples should fall within the comparable range; large gaps flag inconsistent assumptions or stale comps.
