
# Profitability & Efficiency Ratios

## Profitability (earnings relative to revenue, assets, equity)

**Return on Equity (ROE)** = Net Income / Total Shareholders' Equity. How effectively management uses equity; should exceed cost of equity. Benchmarks: <8% below average, 8–15% acceptable, 15–25% good, >25% excellent. High leverage inflates ROE — use DuPont decomposition (ROE = Margin × Turnover × Leverage).

**Return on Assets (ROA)** = Net Income / Total Assets. Efficiency of assets generating profit; asset-light firms run higher. Compare within industry only. Benchmarks: <3% below average, 3–6% acceptable, 6–12% good, >12% excellent.

**Gross Margin** = (Revenue − COGS) / Revenue. Production efficiency and pricing power; declines signal competitive pressure or cost inflation. Industry ranges: Software/SaaS 70–85%, Financial Services 50–70%, Retail 25–45%, Manufacturing 20–40%, Grocery 25–30%.

**Operating Margin** = Operating Income / Revenue. Operational efficiency after operating expenses; excludes interest and taxes. Benchmarks: <5% below average, 5–15% acceptable, 15–25% good, >25% excellent.

**Net Margin** = Net Income / Revenue. Bottom-line profitability after all expenses; affected by tax strategy, capital structure, one-time items. Benchmarks: <3% below average, 3–10% acceptable, 10–20% good, >20% excellent.

## Efficiency (how effectively assets and operations are used)

**Asset Turnover** = Revenue / Total Assets. Revenue per dollar of assets; inversely related to margins (DuPont). Industry ranges: Retail 2.0–3.0, Manufacturing 0.8–1.5, Utilities 0.3–0.5, Technology 0.5–1.0.

**Inventory Turnover** = COGS / Average Inventory. Speed inventory sells; low = overstock/obsolescence risk. Benchmarks: <4x slow, 4–8x average, 8–12x efficient, >12x very efficient.

**Receivables Turnover** = Revenue / Accounts Receivable. Efficiency of credit and collections. Benchmarks: <6x slow, 6–10x average, 10–15x efficient, >15x very efficient.

**Days Sales Outstanding (DSO)** = 365 / Receivables Turnover. Average days to collect after a sale; compare against payment terms. Benchmarks: <30 days excellent, 30–45 good, 45–60 acceptable, >60 concern.

Always compare within industry, review 3–5 year trends, and pair with liquidity/leverage and valuation ratios for a complete picture.
