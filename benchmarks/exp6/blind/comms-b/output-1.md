# Internal Communications Package: Expense Policy Update

---

## 1. All-Hands Announcement (Email from CFO)

**To:** All Employees
**From:** [CFO Name], Chief Financial Officer
**Subject:** Expense policy changes starting August 1 — what's changing and why

Team,

Starting **August 1**, we're changing how travel expenses work. Some of this will make your life easier. One part of it will take away flexibility some of you currently use. I want to walk through both honestly, because I'd rather you hear the full picture from me than piece it together from a policy doc.

**What's changing:**

- **Meals go to a per-diem model.** For trips under 5 days, you'll get a flat daily meal allowance instead of submitting itemized receipts: **$75/day for domestic travel, $110/day for international travel**. No more saving receipts, no more itemizing who ordered what.
- **Alcohol is no longer reimbursable.** This applies across the board, including client and team meals. We know this is the part that will land as a real loss of flexibility for people who currently expense a glass of wine with a client dinner — more on why below.
- **Airfare must be booked through the corporate travel tool.** If you book on your personal card instead, it will only be reimbursed if you got pre-approval first. Spontaneous personal-card bookings for airfare are no longer reimbursable after the fact.
- **60-day transition window.** Any expense report for travel that already happened, submitted within 30 days of that trip, will still be honored under the old rules during the 60 days after launch. This is to make sure nobody who's mid-trip or just got back gets caught in the switch.

**Why we're doing this:**

Finance ran a 2-month pilot of the per-diem and booking-tool model with the sales org. The results: reimbursements were processed **40% faster**, and **92%** of pilot participants said they were satisfied with the new process. That's a real, measured improvement in how fast people got their money back and how much friction they felt — not a hunch, and not a mandate we're rolling out blind.

To be straight with you: the pilot tested the per-diem and booking-tool changes. It did not specifically test the alcohol exclusion in isolation, so I won't claim data support for that piece — that change is a cost and policy decision, not something the pilot proved out. I'd rather tell you that than dress it up as something it isn't.

**What we're still working out:**
There are a handful of details the policy team is still finalizing — how per-diem applies to trips of 5 days or longer, the exact process and turnaround for pre-approval on personal-card airfare bookings, and how domestic vs. international is defined for corner cases (e.g., a trip that crosses both). We'll get you concrete answers before August 1, and the FAQ below will be updated as those land.

Your manager has talking points and can help with day-to-day questions. HR and Finance will share the full updated policy document and the corporate travel tool walkthrough separately before launch.

Thanks for bearing with a change that helps some of you and costs others a bit of flexibility. That trade-off is real, and I didn't want to pretend otherwise.

[CFO Name]

## 3. Manager Talking Points

**Purpose:** Equip team leads to field questions calmly and consistently. Use your judgment on tone — this should feel like a conversation, not a script read aloud.

**Core message to land:**
This is a real trade-off, not a spin job. People get faster reimbursements and less paperwork on meals; people lose the ability to expense alcohol and to book airfare spontaneously on a personal card. Both things are true at once — don't undersell the downside.

**If someone pushes back on the alcohol rule:**
- Acknowledge it directly: "Yeah, that one's a real change for people who take clients out. I'm not going to pretend it isn't."
- Be honest that the pilot data (40% faster, 92% satisfaction) was about the per-diem and booking process, not a study of the alcohol rule specifically — don't imply the data justifies that piece, because it doesn't.
- If pressed on rationale, be honest you don't have a data-backed answer beyond "cost and policy decision" — don't invent one.

**If someone asks "why now" or "why us":**
- The sales org piloted this for two months and it worked operationally (faster money back, high satisfaction). This is a company-wide rollout of something already tested, not an experiment starting from zero.

**If someone has a trip booked for late July that runs into August:**
- Point them to the 60-day transition: reports for travel already completed, filed within 30 days of the trip, are still honored under old rules for 60 days post-launch. If they're unsure whether their specific trip qualifies, escalate to Finance rather than guessing — don't answer edge cases yourself.

**If someone asks about trips 5 days or longer, or the pre-approval process for personal-card airfare:**
- Say plainly: "That's not finalized yet — policy team is working on it, and we'll have an answer before August 1." Do not guess or improvise an answer.

**What NOT to do:**
- Don't frame this as all-upside. It isn't, and people will see through it.
- Don't promise specifics on things not yet decided (5+ day trips, pre-approval turnaround times, edge-case country definitions).
- Don't personally approve or deny exceptions — route those to Finance/HR.

## 4. FAQ

**1. When does this take effect?**
August 1 (the 1st of next month). Trips booked or taken before then follow the old rules, subject to the 60-day transition window described below.

**2. Do I still need to save meal receipts?**
Not for trips under 5 days — you'll get the flat per-diem ($75/day domestic, $110/day international) regardless of what you actually spend, and you don't need to itemize or submit receipts for those meals.

**3. What about meals on trips of 5 days or longer?**
Not yet defined. The policy team hasn't finalized whether longer trips use the per-diem, revert to itemized receipts, or use some blended approach. We'll update this FAQ once that's decided — don't assume either way in the meantime.

**4. Can I still expense a drink with a client dinner?**
No. Alcohol is not reimbursable under the new policy, for any meal type, client or internal. This is one of the real trade-offs of this change — we're not going to pretend it isn't a loss of flexibility for people who used this regularly.

**5. What if I already booked flights on my personal card for a trip after August 1?**
The policy as described applies going forward from August 1; whether pre-existing personal-card bookings made before the announcement need pre-approval retroactively, or are grandfathered, is a detail the policy team needs to clarify. Check with Finance before assuming either outcome.

**6. How do I get pre-approval for a personal-card airfare booking?**
The exact process (who approves, how far in advance you need to ask, what qualifies as a valid reason) is still being finalized by the policy team. Don't book on a personal card assuming it'll be covered until that process is published.

**7. Does the "must book through the corporate travel tool" rule apply to hotels, rail, or rental cars too, or just airfare?**
As described, this rule specifically covers airfare. Whether it extends to other travel categories hasn't been addressed — treat other booking types as unchanged until you hear otherwise.

**8. What's the 60-day transition period, exactly?**
If you traveled and submit your expense report within 30 days of that trip, it will still be processed under the old rules (itemized meal receipts, alcohol reimbursable, no travel-tool requirement) for 60 days after the August 1 launch. After that 60-day window closes, all expense reports — regardless of when the travel occurred — follow the new policy.

**9. Why is the company making this change?**
Finance piloted the per-diem and mandatory-booking-tool model with the sales org for two months. Results: reimbursements processed 40% faster, and 92% of participants reported satisfaction with the new process. This rollout extends that tested model company-wide. (Note: the alcohol exclusion wasn't a variable specifically isolated in the pilot — that piece is a separate cost/policy call, not something the pilot data directly proves.)

**10. Is the per-diem paid regardless of whether I spend the full amount, and do I keep the difference?**
Not specified in what's been announced. Whether unused per-diem is kept by the employee, and how that's taxed or reported, is something the policy team needs to confirm before launch. Don't assume you keep the surplus until that's confirmed.

**11. How is "domestic" vs. "international" defined for a trip that crosses both (e.g., a connecting flight through another country, or a multi-country itinerary)?**
Not yet defined. Policy team needs to specify how mixed-itinerary trips are classified for per-diem purposes.
