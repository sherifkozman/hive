# Expense Policy Change — Internal Communications Package

---

## 1. All-Hands Announcement (Email from CFO)

**To:** All Employees
**From:** [CFO Name], Chief Financial Officer
**Subject:** Expense policy changes starting August 1 — what's changing and why

Hi all,

Starting **August 1**, we're changing how travel expenses work. Here's the short version, then the details.

**What's changing:**

- **Meals on trips under 5 days** are now covered by a flat per-diem instead of itemized receipts: **$75/day domestic, $110/day international**. No more saving receipts, no more itemizing — you get the per-diem amount regardless of what you actually spent.
- **Alcohol is no longer reimbursable**, on any trip, of any length.
- **Airfare must be booked through [corporate travel tool]**. Booking on a personal card will only be reimbursed if you get pre-approval first.
- **Old expense reports still count.** If you travel before August 1 and submit within 30 days of your trip, the old process applies. We're giving everyone a **60-day transition window** to get reports in under the old rules — after that, the new policy is the only policy.

**Why we're doing this:**

We piloted the per-diem and mandatory-booking changes with the sales org for two months. The results: reimbursements went out **40% faster**, and **92%** of people in the pilot said they preferred it to the old process. That's a real, measured improvement, not a guess — and it's the reason we're rolling it out company-wide rather than tweaking further.

**Where this is a real trade-off, not just a win:**

We're not going to pretend everyone comes out ahead. If you're used to expensing a meal with a client that runs over the per-diem — or a drink with dinner — you'll have less flexibility than before. The per-diem is a flat amount either way: if you spend less, you keep the difference; if you spend more, you're covering it yourself. That's the honest trade-off in exchange for faster, simpler processing for everyone.

**What you need to do:**

- Nothing right now. The policy takes effect August 1.
- Starting then, book all airfare through [corporate travel tool]. If you have a specific reason to book elsewhere, request pre-approval before you buy the ticket, not after.
- Keep expensing meals as usual for any trip already in progress before August 1, or submitted within 30 days of travel.

Your manager has talking points and an FAQ to help answer questions — and if something's not covered there, ask. We'll keep updating the FAQ as questions come in.

Thanks for bearing with the transition.

[CFO Name]

## 3. Manager Talking Points

*For team leads fielding questions from your team. Goal: you should be able to explain the change and its rationale without reading from a script.*

**The headline:**
Starting August 1, expense reporting for travel gets simpler in most ways and stricter in a couple of specific ways. It's not a cost-cutting exercise dressed up as a process improvement — it's a real process improvement (proven in a 2-month pilot), with one real cost-cutting piece (no more alcohol reimbursement) bundled in honestly.

**What's changing, in plain terms:**
1. Meals on short trips (under 5 days) become a flat daily amount — no receipts to track or submit. $75/day in the US, $110/day abroad.
2. Alcohol is off the reimbursable list entirely, period, regardless of trip length.
3. Airfare booking is now expected to go through [corporate travel tool]. Personal-card bookings need pre-approval before purchase — after-the-fact requests will be turned down.
4. There's a 60-day transition: any trip that happened before August 1, expensed within 30 days of travel, still goes through the old process.

**Why we're making this change:**
Finance piloted the per-diem and booking-tool changes with the sales org for two months. Reimbursements came back 40% faster, and 92% of people in that pilot preferred it. This isn't a hunch — it's a rollout of something that was tested and worked.

**What to say if someone pushes back:**
- *"I expense more than $75/day on meals normally."* — Be honest: yes, some people will get less flexibility than before, especially on client dinners or higher cost-of-living cities. The per-diem is flat either way — if you spend less you keep it, if you spend more it's on you. That's a real trade-off in exchange for not dealing with receipts and faster payouts.
- *"Why is alcohol excluded?"* — It's a straightforward policy simplification, not a judgment call about anyone's specific past expenses. Applies uniformly, no exceptions.
- *"What if I already booked something through a travel agent I like?"* — Get pre-approval for personal-card bookings before you buy, not after. If you're not sure whether something qualifies, ask before booking, not after the trip.
- *"What about a trip that's already in progress?"* — If it was booked/traveled before August 1 and submitted within 30 days of the trip, the old rules apply, inside the 60-day window.

**What you don't need to have an answer for:**
Some specifics haven't been nailed down publicly yet — trips of 5+ days, how per-diem interacts with company-hosted meals, what "pre-approval" actually requires procedurally. Don't guess or invent an answer — point people to the FAQ, and if it's not there, tell them you'll find out and follow up. Flag anything you can't answer to [Finance/Policy contact] so the FAQ can be updated.

## 4. FAQ

*Question:* When does this take effect?
*Answer:* August 1 (the 1st of next month). Trips before that date, expensed within 30 days of travel, are still processed under the old rules, and that grace period runs for 60 days total.

*Question:* What exactly is the per-diem amount, and when does it apply?
*Answer:* $75/day for domestic trips and $110/day for international trips, for meals on trips under 5 days. It replaces itemized meal receipts — you get the flat amount regardless of what you actually spent.

*Question:* What happens on trips that are 5 days or longer — is there still a per-diem, or do I need receipts?
*Answer:* The facts we have only specify per-diem for trips under 5 days. What applies to longer trips hasn't been stated — the policy team needs to clarify this rather than us guessing.

*Question:* If I spend less than the per-diem, do I keep the difference? What if I go over?
*Answer:* The per-diem is a flat amount: if you spend less, you keep the rest; if you spend more, the extra comes out of your own pocket. It's not a reimbursement of actual spend anymore, it's a fixed allowance.

*Question:* Is alcohol never reimbursable now, even for client dinners or team events?
*Answer:* Correct as stated — alcohol is no longer reimbursable, full stop. Whether client entertainment or team events are budgeted differently (e.g., through a separate team budget rather than individual expense reports) isn't covered in what's been announced, so check with your manager or finance if that situation comes up.

*Question:* Do I have to use the corporate travel tool for hotels and other travel too, or just flights?
*Answer:* As announced, the mandatory-booking requirement applies specifically to airfare. Whether hotels, rental cars, or other travel bookings are affected hasn't been stated — don't assume it extends beyond airfare until the policy team confirms.

*Question:* How do I get pre-approval to book airfare on a personal card?
*Answer:* Pre-approval must happen before you book, not after the fact — after-the-trip requests won't be honored. The specific process (who approves it, what form or channel to use, turnaround time) hasn't been detailed yet; that's something the policy team needs to publish before this becomes practical to follow.

*Question:* I have a trip already booked for after August 1 — does the old policy apply since I booked it earlier?
*Answer:* Based on what's been announced, the transition rule is tied to when you traveled and submitted, not when you booked. If the trip itself happens on or after August 1, the new policy applies. If this creates a problem for trips booked before the announcement, flag it — the policy team hasn't explicitly addressed booking-date edge cases.

*Question:* Why is the company doing this — is it just about cutting costs?
*Answer:* Finance ran a 2-month pilot with the sales org before rolling this out company-wide: reimbursements were 40% faster and 92% of pilot participants preferred the new process. The per-diem and booking-tool changes are backed by that data. The one piece that's a straightforward cost reduction, not a process improvement, is the alcohol exclusion — that's being communicated honestly as a cost decision, not spun as something else.

*Question:* Where do I submit per-diem claims — is it the same expense system as before?
*Answer:* Not specified in what's been announced. The policy team needs to confirm whether per-diem is claimed through the existing expense system, paid automatically based on trip dates, or something else.
